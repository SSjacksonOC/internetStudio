Fox:

 The fox crushes a single wing under their paw, "Swear? To whom? You are sworn to me and only me. Me! All ME! ONLY ME! No—so ungrateful! What other hand is there? Me—ME! It is from my hand that you feed!" and with a pounce, sharp teeth envelope the bird's head. In a single shake, a resounding 'snap' can be heard as head and soul are divorced from body. Making sure to impale the heart, the Fox leaves the ungrateful employee with not even the grace of a hole dug for them.

You should not stay. There is too much too see...
